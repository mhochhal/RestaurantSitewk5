Created from the dreams by Barbara and Frenk Melton in the 1960's, it was brought to life in 1965 after 3 hard years of slowly building a little at a time. It was a hit from the beginning, bringing in customers from a far. The Blondie Burger was the most sought out burger on the gulfcoast. In 1969, destruction hit and tore the Diner to shreds. Hurricane Camille brought on a screaching halt for the Melton's family. After Camille, Barbara and Frank went to shelters and food banks to bring people their Blondie Burgers. They even created a new burger called the Happy Food Burger. Created to bring a smile to all the faces after Camille, it is a burger that is featured with a "smiley face" made of ketchup! People waited patiently daily for when the Melton's would walk through the doors and bring in hot, steamy food. It took 4 more years to get the Diner running again. The customers that had went to Destiney's the few years before never strayed. The opening days brought in over a thousand customers a day. In 2005, on the 40th Anniversary, the Melton's named their granddaughter Christina and husband Matthew Hochhalter the new owners. In 2007,
the Hochhalter's had a daughter and even named her Destiney after the Diner!
